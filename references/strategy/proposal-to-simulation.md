# Proposal To Simulation

Use when the user wants to show a future state, service outcome, spatial change, operational change, or product effect that a PPT cannot demonstrate.

## Contract

```text
simulation route:
- original state:
- proposed intervention:
- visible transformation:
- user controls:
- assumptions:
- what must remain truthful:
- fallback still:
```

## Routes

### Street / Lighting / Architecture

Input: street photo, facade photo, site plan, render, mood reference.

Transform:

- original daylight/dark street remains available
- new lighting state appears through toggle, scrub, staged reveal, or scroll
- focus points: pole, facade, sign, sidewalk, entrance, tree, safety zone
- optional heat/beam overlays show lighting intent, not fake engineering accuracy

Controls:

- before/after slider
- night/day toggle
- click-to-focus detail
- pan/zoom map or photo
- chapter scrub from "current" to "intervention" to "experience"

Reject if:

- original disappears
- generated lighting is presented as measured lux data
- the result is just a pretty night image with no inspectable intervention

### Interior / Retail / Exhibition

Transform floor plan or photo into zones:

- path, display, lighting, dwell point, conversion point
- user can enter focus mode for each zone
- generated perspective plates may support atmosphere
- exact dimensions remain labeled as assumptions unless sourced

### Service / Operations / Consulting Proposal

Transform process into a simulated room, flow, or mission control:

- before bottleneck
- intervention mechanism
- after outcome
- proof anchors from the proposal
- user can step through events or drag the timeline

### Product Outcome / Before-After

Transform product claim into visible state:

- untreated vs treated
- packed vs assembled
- closed vs exploded
- static vs active
- small view vs inspected detail

## Truth Labels

Use labels such as:

- "concept visualization"
- "assumption: lighting direction"
- "source photo preserved"
- "not photometric simulation"
- "generated future-state plate"

The point is persuasive clarity, not fake certainty.
